Part 1: Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

When holding the camera close, the pencils or rays from the nose and other parts of the face arrive at the point of the camera at steep angles. This makes them appear much larger than the ears or sides of the head. It exaggerates perspective and causes the "big nose". When stepping back and zooming in, the rays from all parts of the face arrive nearly parallel. Therefore, the proportions look more natural.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

From further away with zoom, the pencils of rays from near and far buildings reach the camera almost in parallel, which visually compresses the whole scence. This makes distances look comparitively short. Up close, the rays spread at steeper angles, so nearby objects appear a lot larger while more distant objects tend to shrink. This makes the stree look stretched.
